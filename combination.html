linear combination is a mathematical concept used in various fields such as algebra, geometry, and vector spaces. In simple terms, a linear combination involves adding together multiple vectors (or functions, polynomials, etc.) that have been multiplied by coefficients (which are either scalars or constants).
Definition of Linear Combination
Given a set of vectors v1, v2, . . . ,vn in a vector space, a linear combination of these vectors is an expression of the form:
w = c1v1 + c2v2 + . . . + cnvn
Where c1, c2, . . . ,cn are scalars (real numbers, complex numbers, etc.).

Commutative Property
The commutative property states that the order in which vectors are added does not affect the result. In other words, the sum of two or more vectors remains the same regardless of the order in which they are added.
